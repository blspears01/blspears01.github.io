Summary

I got my Driver's Permit in July, and I got relatively consistent hours up until the start of school. Once school started, I became more busy, and I lost the habit of driving. I made driving my main Passion Project because I thought it would motivate me to get back on track. I think it worked really well because I made up for the lost time and drove nearly every day. For my drives, I used an Audi A4, a Honda CR-V, and a Ford Focus. I drove all around Boulder, Lafayette, and Broomfield.

Progress

I built a lot of skills in the 17 hours that I drove. I started out being very uncomfortable at all times, but I have definitely grown more familiar with the cars. I can follow the rules of the road without much issue. I am much better at changing lanes and managing my line position. The fastest road that I drove at the beginning of this project had a speed limit of about 40mph. Now, I have driven on US-36 at fast speeds and at busy times. I have also adapted to different weather conditions. I have experienced a little bit of ice, some rain, a lot of sun, and wind. I tried challenging myself with winding roads in addition to regular city roads, in order to prepare myself for more rural driving. I would say that there is a huge difference in the kind of driver I am now compared to me at the beginning of this Passion Project. I have to say that the skill I am most proud of, though, is the J-Turn, which you can see in the Media section.

Result

I am very satisfied with the progress I made as a result of this project. I will continue driving probably for the rest of my life, so I am glad I spent a lot of time working on driving skills. My plan is to keep practicing for my test, until I feel as comfortable as I can behind the wheel.
